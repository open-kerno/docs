We started this blog to share what we learn as we build Kerno.

{/* truncate */}

We will write about technical decisions, tools we use, and problems we solve. Some posts will be short. Others will go deep into a specific topic.

We are a small team moving fast. Not every decision we make is perfect, but we try to be thoughtful about the things that are hard to change later — like data formats, API contracts, and system boundaries.

## What to expect

- **Engineering standards** — How we handle data, naming, and consistency across services.
- **Incident reviews** — What broke, why, and what we changed.
- **Tool evaluations** — What we tried, what we kept, and what we dropped.

## Who writes here

Anyone on the engineering team can publish here. Posts go through a light review before they are published. The goal is to share real context, not polished marketing content.

If you are on the team and want to write something, open a PR against this repo.
